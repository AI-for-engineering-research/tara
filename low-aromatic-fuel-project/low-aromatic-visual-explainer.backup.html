Hydrotreatment pathway — simplified schematic

Atmospheric distillation produces a kerosene cut. Kerosene is treated in a hydrotreater with makeup hydrogen, then separated in a fractionator. Acid gas is handled via amine + Claus. Outputs include HT kerosene and sulfur byproducts.

Key tradeoff
Aromatics reduction + sulfur reduction
Lower aromatics can reduce particulate emissions; sulfur reduction may affect radiative forcing outcomes.
Key tradeoff
Hydrogen demand → added refinery CO₂ emissions
Hydrogen source (SMR vs electrolysis) dominates production-stage CO₂ outcomes and incremental production cost.
Key tradeoff
Incremental production cost (TEA)
Driven by utilities, hydrogen, and unit additions (CAPEX/OPEX) for treatment and sulfur handling.
Key tradeoff
Net climate outcome depends on multiple effects
Contrails + CO₂ + sulfur-related cases; evaluate over geography and uncertainty ranges.

Solvent extraction pathway — simplified schematic

Atmospheric distillation produces a kerosene cut. Kerosene is processed through an extractor with a circulating solvent, followed by washer + stripper stages and solvent recovery with recycled solvent and water loops.

Key tradeoff
Aromatic removal vs solvent circulation
Higher removal typically increases circulation rates and separation duty.
Key tradeoff
Utility demand + solvent losses
Stripper + recovery energy; quantify solvent makeup and emissions implications.
Key tradeoff
Capital cost / process complexity
Extractor/stripper/recovery train adds CAPEX; check scale sensitivity.
Key tradeoff
Co-product aromatics stream
Allocation / market assumptions can affect net economics and outcomes.
