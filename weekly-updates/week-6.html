Teach skill: learning how LLMs work

July 15, 2026

I then used the teach skill on a topic where I actually needed the scaffolding: LLMs. That session set up a small learning workspace and walked me through next-token prediction, training vs. inference, tokens, embeddings, positional information, attention, and practical LLM use from the underlying mechanism.

The conversation is here: LLM teach skill conversation. It also produced a set of lesson files in docs/lessons/LLM lesson, including the opening lesson on next-token prediction, and I saved a handoff summary here: LLM learning workspace handoff. By the end, I had a much clearer mental model of what an LLM is actually doing step by step.

LLM lesson map

01. Next-token machine

Explains the basic generation loop: predict a next token, append it, and repeat.

 02. Tokens are not words

Shows that models process chunks of text, not raw words or characters.

 03. Embeddings

Describes how token IDs become learned vectors the model can compute with.

 04. Position matters

Explains why transformers need position information to understand word order.

 05. Attention

Introduces attention as relevance weighting across the context.

 06. Queries, keys, values

Breaks attention into queries that search, keys that match, and values that carry content.

 07. Multi-head attention

Shows how multiple attention heads track different relationships in parallel.

 08. Layers, residuals, MLPs

Explains the transformer stack: attention, per-token computation, and residual updates.

 09. Training vs. inference

Clarifies that training changes weights, while inference uses fixed weights to generate.

 10. Logits, softmax, sampling

Connects model scores to probabilities and decoding choices.

 11. Hallucinations and grounding

Explains why plausible false answers happen and how external evidence helps.

 12. Practical LLM use

Turns the mechanism into habits for context, grounding, decoding, and verification.
