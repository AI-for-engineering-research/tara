Hallucinations and grounding

Core idea: Hallucinations are often plausible but false continuations; grounding reduces them by adding external evidence to context.

What to remember

  • The model is optimized for plausible continuation, not guaranteed truth.
  • Weak or ambiguous context raises hallucination risk.
  • Grounding means supplying trusted source material.

Why it matters

This explains both why hallucinations happen and why retrieval, tools, and source passages help.

Mental model

Weights alone can produce likely-sounding answers; grounded context constrains what likely means.
