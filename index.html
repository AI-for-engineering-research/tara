Cost Benefit Analysis of Low Aromatic Fuels

Problem:

Aromatics have higher binding energy between atoms compared to aliphatic compounds. Jet fuel with higher aromatics content has higher soot emissions.

Soot emissions have a variety of impacts:

  • Air quality related health impacts
  • Climate impacts due to direct & indirect radiative forcing via contrails

Opportunity:

Reduce aromatics content via refining → Reduce soot emissions → Reduce jet fuel's environmental impact?

Research goal: Cost benefit analysis needed to evaluate advantages and trade-offs of opportunity.

Aromatics Removal Techniques

①

Hydrotreatment

Kerosene is treated with hydrogen in a hydrotreator to reduce aromatics.

②

Solvent Extraction

Aromatics are selectively extracted, then solvent is recovered in a stripper.
